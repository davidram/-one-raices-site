The Renter's Guide

Find the right rental, faster — and avoid common renter mistakes.

1 Know your real budget

Rent is usually recommended to stay under 30% of your gross income — but also factor in utilities, parking, and any pet fees before you fall in love with a place.

2 Gather your documents early

Most landlords ask for proof of income, ID, and references. Having these ready lets you move fast on a great rental before someone else does.

3 Inspect before you sign

Test every faucet and outlet, check for water damage or mold, and take photos of any existing damage on move-in day to protect your deposit.

4 Read the lease carefully

Pay attention to the lease term, renewal terms, who's responsible for repairs, and the exact deposit return policy before you sign anything.
